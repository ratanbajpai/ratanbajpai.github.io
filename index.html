Narative Visualisation     

Examining the differing trend towards a more urbanised society

Author: Kevin Sweeney ( mail )        Tested in Google Chrome

Since detailed records have been available, there has been a general trend towards a more urbanised way of living. The data presented within this visualisation (from the World Bank dataset) details the percentage growth rate in urban population versus the urban population percentage for the years 2005, 2010 and 2015 for all the available countries of the world. Some minor data cleaning has been completed.


This Interactive Slideshow visualisation allows the user to step through the three available years while providing the ability to explore the data at each time point. The visualisation also provides some annotations detailing some interesting findings within the data.


It can be seen from the interactive slideshow that the various regions of the world have widely differing trends towards urbanisation. Some regions are currently quite rural (for example Sub-Saharan Africa) with a significant trend towards urbanisation while other regions are already highly urbanised (for example Middle East & North Africa), possibly due to lack of space or habitable space and are continuing that trend.
